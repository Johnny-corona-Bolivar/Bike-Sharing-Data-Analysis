# 📈 Key Insights & Business Recommendations

The following insights summarize the most important behavioral differences identified between **Annual Members** and **Casual Riders**. Each finding is supported by data visualizations and translated into actionable business recommendations.

---

# 📊 Insight 1 — Average Ride Duration

<p align="center">
<img src="images/average_ride_duration.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

Annual Members complete rides with an average duration of **10.7 minutes**, whereas Casual Riders average **14.3 minutes**, representing a **33.6% longer ride duration**.

## Business Interpretation

This pattern suggests that Members primarily use Cyclistic as a daily transportation service, while Casual Riders are more likely to use bicycles for recreational or leisure purposes.

The longer ride duration among Casual Riders indicates an opportunity to create membership plans tailored to recreational users.

## Business Recommendation

- Design membership plans focused on leisure riders.
- Offer weekend benefits for members.
- Include recreational incentives such as free ride extensions or seasonal promotions.

---

# 📊 Insight 2 — Trips by Rider Type

<p align="center">
<img src="images/trips_by_rider_type.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

Annual Members generated approximately **64% of all trips**, while Casual Riders accounted for **36%**.

| Rider Type | Trips |
|------------|-------:|
| Annual Members | 3,553,497 |
| Casual Riders | 1,999,497 |

## Business Interpretation

Members consistently use the service throughout the year, demonstrating greater customer loyalty and generating higher long-term value.

Although Casual Riders represent a smaller portion of total rides, they remain a significant market segment with strong conversion potential.

## Business Recommendation

- Develop targeted acquisition campaigns focused on active Casual Riders.
- Promote the financial benefits of annual memberships.
- Introduce limited-time membership discounts.

---

# 📊 Insight 3 — Trips by Day of Week

<p align="center">
<img src="images/trips_by_day.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

Casual Riders significantly increase bicycle usage between **Friday and Sunday**, while Annual Members maintain relatively stable usage throughout the week.

## Business Interpretation

Weekend demand suggests that Casual Riders primarily use Cyclistic for leisure activities.

Annual Members display a commuting-oriented usage pattern with consistent weekday activity.

## Business Recommendation

- Launch weekend-focused marketing campaigns.
- Offer promotional memberships during weekends.
- Create referral programs targeting leisure users.

---

# 📊 Insight 4 — Seasonal Usage

<p align="center">
<img src="images/trips_by_season.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

Both rider groups increase bicycle usage during **Summer** and **Fall**, although the seasonal effect is considerably stronger among Casual Riders.

## Business Interpretation

Seasonality has a significant impact on service demand.

Peak riding periods provide the ideal opportunity to convert Casual Riders into long-term members.

## Business Recommendation

- Increase marketing investment during Summer and Fall.
- Launch seasonal membership campaigns.
- Offer promotional incentives during low-demand months to reduce seasonality.

---

# 📊 Insight 5 — Trips by Hour

<p align="center">
<img src="images/trips_by_hour.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

Annual Members show two distinct demand peaks:

- 7:00 AM – 9:00 AM
- 3:00 PM – 7:00 PM

Casual Riders display a much more evenly distributed riding pattern throughout the day.

## Business Interpretation

Members primarily use bicycles for commuting between home and work.

Casual Riders exhibit a recreational usage pattern with greater flexibility in riding times.

## Business Recommendation

- Promote memberships during commuting hours.
- Optimize bicycle availability during peak demand.
- Monitor bicycle shortages at peak times.

---

# 📊 Insight 6 — Bike Type Preference

<p align="center">
<img src="images/bike_type.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

Both customer segments clearly prefer **Electric Bikes** over Classic Bikes.

## Business Interpretation

Electric bicycles represent a key component of customer satisfaction.

Growing demand may require operational adjustments to ensure sufficient fleet availability.

## Business Recommendation

- Evaluate electric fleet capacity.
- Consider expanding the electric bike fleet.
- Encourage Classic Bike usage through promotional campaigns if fleet expansion is not feasible.

---

# 📊 Insight 7 — Most Used Station

<p align="center">
<img src="images/top_station.png" width="900">
</p>

## Key Finding

**Canal St & Madison St** is the most frequently used station for both Annual Members and Casual Riders.

## Business Interpretation

This station appears to be a strategic transportation hub within the Cyclistic network.

Its popularity may be influenced by location, connectivity, nearby workplaces, or public transportation.

## Business Recommendation

- Analyze station demand by time of day.
- Evaluate bicycle availability.
- Improve redistribution strategies.
- Consider increasing station capacity if demand continues to grow.

# 📋 Executive Summary

This analysis identified clear behavioral differences between Annual Members and Casual Riders.

Annual Members primarily use Cyclistic bicycles for daily commuting, resulting in shorter ride durations, consistent weekday usage, and predictable peak demand during commuting hours.

Conversely, Casual Riders demonstrate longer ride durations, higher weekend activity, and increased seasonal demand, suggesting that their trips are primarily recreational.

These findings indicate a strong opportunity to convert Casual Riders into Annual Members through targeted marketing campaigns, seasonal promotions, and membership plans tailored to leisure-oriented users.

Implementing these recommendations has the potential to increase customer retention, improve profitability, and support Cyclistic's long-term growth strategy.

---

# 📖 Storytelling

Although both customer groups use the same bicycle-sharing system, the analysis reveals two fundamentally different customer behaviors.

Annual Members rely on Cyclistic as a transportation solution integrated into their daily routines.

Casual Riders, however, view the service primarily as a recreational activity.

Understanding these behavioral differences allows Cyclistic to move beyond generic marketing campaigns and instead develop personalized strategies tailored to each customer segment.

Rather than attracting entirely new customers, the company has an opportunity to increase profitability by strengthening relationships with existing Casual Riders and encouraging them to become Annual Members.

Data transforms assumptions into evidence, enabling more informed business decisions and creating measurable opportunities for sustainable growth.
